Me as a Computational Engineer

Embarking on my academic journey at the prestigious Technical University of Munich (TUM) in Germany was a defining chapter in my pursuit of knowledge. Completing my Master of Computational Mechanics with distinction, boasting a stellar GPA of 3.6/4.0, was a testament to my dedication and academic prowess.

The pinnacle of my academic endeavor was my Master's Thesis, where I delved into the intricate realm of fluid dynamics by investigating the Turbulent Horseshoe Vortex based on Large Eddy Simulation (LES) data of flow around a scoured bridge pier. This groundbreaking research not only challenged my intellect but also contributed significantly to the advancement of this field.

Throughout my tenure, I engaged in a spectrum of coursework that enriched my understanding of computational mechanics. From mastering the art of Turbulent Flow Modeling to unraveling the complexities of Advanced Fluid Mechanics, every course was a stepping stone towards excellence. Delving into Computational Fluid Dynamics, Computational Material Modeling, and the Finite Element Method further broadened my academic horizons, equipping me with the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in the dynamic field of computational mechanics.
